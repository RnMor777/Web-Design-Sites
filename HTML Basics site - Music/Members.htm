Freddie Mercury:

Freddie Mercury was born on September 5th, 1946 in England. His original name was Freddie Bulsara. He died on November 24th, 1991 from Bronchopneumonia. He was the age of 45. He was part of the band Queen from 1969-1991. His voice range was between a low bass and high soprano. This totaled to a 4 octave voice range. In 2001, like the rest of the band he was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame. In 2009, he was elected as the greatest rock singer of all time by Classic Rock readers.

Brian May:

Brian May was born on July 19th, 1947 in England. He currently is 69 years old. In the band Queen, he was the lead guitarist and used a guitar that he made with his father. He called this guitar the "Red Special". In 2005, a Planet Rock poll voted him as the 7th greatest guitarist of all time. Before joining the band Queen in 1970, he formed his own band called Smile. Besides playing the guitar, he was the lower-range back-up vocalist.

Roger Taylor:

Roger Taylor was born on July 26th, 1949 in England. He is currently 67. Like May, he was part of the band Smile before joining the band Queen. He was the lead drummer for Queen but also played keyboards and guitars. He composed around at least one track of all of their albums. In 2005, he was voted as the eighth-greatest drummer in classic rock by Planet Rock.

John Deacon:

John Deacon was born on August 19th, 1951. He is currently the age of 65. Following the death of Freddie Mercury, he retired in 1997 after performing a couple times with the rest of the band. He was the leading bass guitarist for the band of Queen. His first band that he joined was The Opposition when he was 14. He was the last member of Queen to join. He is the only member to have not had a vocal parts in any songs.
